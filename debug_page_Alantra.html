Associate - Corporate Development (M&A) & Strategy

Close ×

ALANTRA is a global investment banking, asset management and portfolio advisory firm focusing on the mid-market with offices in 22 countries across Europe, the US, Latin America and Asia. With over 500 professionals, the firm is publicly listed since 2015 with a €500Mn market capitalization as of October 2022. Its Investment Banking division employs over 300 professionals, providing independent advice on M&A, debt advisory, financial restructuring, and capital markets transactions. In Asset Management, Alantra offers its clients unique access to a wide range of investment strategies (direct investments, fund of funds, co-investments and secondaries) in six highly specialized asset management classes (private equity, active funds, private debt, infrastructure, real estate and venture capital) as well as private wealth management services. Alantra’s portfolio advisory team is the leading portfolio advisory unit in Europe, with over 160 seasoned professionals across the UK, Spain, Ireland, Italy, Portugal and Greece dedicated to transaction execution, structuring, pricing, modelling and data enhancement in relation to credit portfolios and banking platform. For more information, visit www.alantra.com

Based in Madrid but with full international exposure, you will work in a highly dynamic, inspiring and challenging environment and become a key member of our Corporate Development & Strategy team.

You will be part of a multidisciplinary team of five professionals led by the Group COO and work closely with Alantra’s top management, including the Executive Chairman & Group CEO and CFO´s business units.

Your day-to-day tasks will contain a wide range of activities including, among others:

• Corporate development (in-house M&A team): Execution of all sorts of transactions (acquisitions, capital increases, JVs, etc.) – more than 30 deals

analyzed/executed during 2023). You will analyze companies and draft and review execution documents and other deliverables such as proposals, IM’s, financial models, due diligence and legal contracts. This position will also support the senior team members in meetings with counterparts, deal negotiations, valuation, transaction structuring etc

• Strategy: Prepare and execute, together with the business divisions, the strategic plans for each of the different business units, including analysis of KPIs and

business performance, market dynamics, competitors trends, etc.

You will be in charge of preparing presentations for the senior management of the firm (Board of Directors and Executive Committees).

• Investor relations: support Head of IR attending events with investors, preparing presentations and results and campaign material.

You will need to have exceptional financial modeling and writing skills, a solid experience in financial statements analysis and accounting, experience with legal documents (NDA’s, LOI’s, MoU’s, SHA’s and SPA’s) as well as outstanding communication capacity and people management skills.

Finally, you should act as a role model to junior team members through permanent coaching and training to challenge, develop and empower them. You should also be able to identify and organize junior resources to meet and deliver deadlines focusing on the highest priorities.

Apply

Requirements

What you will bring:

• 3-5 years of strategic consulting, investment banking, private equity, corporate

development or transaction services experience from tier 1 firms. The professional

category will be determined based on candidates’ experience.

• Strong academic background from top tier Business or Engineering schools.

• Strong quantitative/analytical skills – proficiency with financial models and

knowledge of valuation methodologies.

• High technical skills, ability to digest large amount of complex information.

• Demonstrated strategic and creative thinking.

• Ability to work collaboratively in a team and create an inclusive environment with

people at all levels of an organization

• Capability to drive an independent workstream in the context of a broader team

project

• Committed, rigorous, detail-oriented attitude combined with ability to show

initiative and to take decisions.

• Highest standards of personal conduct, professional performance and business

ethics.

• Complete fluency in English is mandatory (fluency in Spanish is not mandatory).

• Investor mindset and strong interest in the financial industry (investment banking,

asset management, private capital, etc.)

• Comfort with ambiguous, ever-changing situations

Strong financial knowledge (P&L, BS and CF) and understanding of financial

statements.

• Strong academic background from top tier Universities or Engineering Schools.

• Commitment, rigorous detail-oriented, attitude and ability to show initiative at work,

with the ability to contribute with your own ideas.

• Ability to manage several assignments at once and work equally effectively as an

individual and as part of a team.

• Capable of working under pressure and in a context of shifting priorities.

• Impeccable communication and presentation skills (verbal and written).

• Proficiency in the use of Excel, Power Point, Word and Outlook.

About the Role

We are seeking a highly organized, proactive, and versatile Personal Assistant & Office Manager to support our dynamic team in Dublin. This hybrid role combines high-level administrative support to senior directors with comprehensive office management duties, ensuring smooth day-to-day operations and a professional, welcoming workplace

Key Responsibilities

Personal Assistant Duties

  • Provide extensive PA support to a team of 4 (Managing Director x2 / Director x2), including full diary and calendar management
  • Manage monthly expense submissions via Concur and weekly timesheet processing through SAP
  • Deliver high-quality document support including formatting presentations to company/client branding, processing edits efficiently, preparing print-ready documents, and handling printing/binding ahead of meetings
  • Coordinate all aspects of business travel arrangements: flights, trains, accommodation, visas, meeting venues, and dining reservations
  • Assist with event planning and coordination, including monthly internal social events such as team lunches and drinks
  • Maintain and update CRM databases by managing contacts and ensuring data accuracy
  • Undertake ad-hoc administrative tasks as needed, such as filing, scanning, photocopying, archiving, and running general errands
  • Provide occasional support to the wider Dublin team as required

Office Manager Duties

  • Manage all incoming calls to the Dublin mainline and wider team, taking messages and scheduling calls efficiently
  • Oversee front-of-house operations, greeting visitors warmly and managing reception duties
  • Coordinate catering and refreshments for meetings and events; ensure reception, meeting rooms, and kitchen areas are clean, tidy, and well-stocked at all times
  • Maintain marketing materials, newspapers, and industry publications in public areas
  • Set up and troubleshoot IT and video conferencing equipment as needed
  • Handle all incoming and outgoing mail, couriers, and deliveries
  • Manage office supplies inventory and place orders for stationery, refreshments, and other essentials
  • Oversee supplier contracts and manage budgets, ensuring cost-effectiveness and high-quality service
  • Regularly check and maintain office equipment such as printers, scanners, and photocopiers
  • Coordinate office repairs, maintenance, and liaise with building management
  • Provide basic IT support for staff before escalating to the IT helpdesk; collaborate with the IT department on equipment management
  • Manage office floor plans, desk moves, onboarding and offboarding processes, including health and safety inductions
  • Lead office health & safety management: ensure compliance, maintain first aiders and fire marshals, perform regular inspections, and oversee fire safety equipment maintenance
  • Complete risk assessments and safety checklists for office and contractors
  • Communicate Dublin-wide updates affecting transport or office access, ensuring staff are well-informed of local events impacting the workplace
Apply

Requirements

  • Proven experience as a Personal Assistant and Office Manager supporting senior executives
  • Exceptional organizational skills with a keen eye for detail
  • Strong communication and interpersonal skills, capable of representing the company professionally
  • Proficiency with Concur, SAP, Microsoft Office Suite, and CRM systems is preferred
  • Ability to multitask and adapt to a fast-paced, evolving environment
  • A proactive and solution-oriented mindset with a friendly, approachable demeanour
